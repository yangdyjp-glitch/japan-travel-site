Overview

From the roots of myth to contemporary art islands.

This is one of the most distinctive routes in the collection. It connects two layers of Japan: ancient belief and regional culture in Izumo, Matsue, and Iwami Ginzan, followed by contemporary art, architecture, and island regeneration in Naoshima and Teshima.

The journey is strongest for discerning adult travelers, art and design students, architects, collectors, museum-minded travelers, and small custom groups looking beyond the standard Japan circuit.

Best fit

  • Repeat Japan travelers seeking rare regions and deeper interpretation.
  • Art, architecture, design, and garden enthusiasts.
  • Private groups and mature cultural travelers.
  • Not ideal for first-time travelers who only want famous landmarks.

Highlights

Rare regions paired with rich art content.

Izumo Mythology

Izumo Taisha and Inasa-no-hama introduce Shinto belief, origin stories, and the cultural idea of connection.

Matsue and Gardens

Matsue Castle, tea culture, Lake Shinji, Adachi Museum, and Japanese garden interpretation support the route's elegance.

Iwami Ginzan

The mining heritage area adds world-heritage depth and a story of trade, resource culture, and regional history.

Naoshima

Chichu Art Museum, Benesse House, Lee Ufan Museum, and Yayoi Kusama works make the art-island story immediately legible.

Teshima

Teshima Art Museum offers a distinctive meeting point of art, architecture, landscape, and silence.

Kyoto Comparison

Kyoto gardens and temples help compare classical Japanese aesthetics with Setouchi contemporary art.

Day by day

11-day sample itinerary.

D1

Arrive in Tokyo

Private transfer, hotel check-in, welcome dinner, and introduction to the mythology-to-art route narrative.

D2

Tokyo art and architecture

teamLab, Ueno museums, Mori Art Museum, Omotesando, Ginza, or Roppongi architecture walk depending on group interests.

D3

Tokyo to Izumo

Fly to Izumo, visit Izumo Taisha and Inasa-no-hama, then stay in Izumo or Matsue.

D4

Matsue culture day

Matsue Castle, castle town streets, tea culture, Lake Shinji views, and Sanin regional cuisine.

D5

Adachi Museum and Yuushien

Explore Japanese garden aesthetics at Adachi Museum, then visit Yuushien or nearby traditional landscapes.

D6

Iwami Ginzan to Okayama or Uno

Explore Iwami Ginzan and Omori town, then continue toward Okayama or Uno for the Setouchi island portion.

D7

Naoshima art island

Ferry from Uno to Naoshima. Visit Chichu Art Museum, Benesse House, Lee Ufan Museum, and key outdoor works.

D8

Teshima or Naoshima depth

Visit Teshima Art Museum and island landscapes, or deepen Naoshima with the Art House Project before returning to Okayama or Kyoto.

D9

Kyoto gardens and temples

Ryoan-ji, Ginkaku-ji, Nanzen-ji, or selected gardens to compare classical space with contemporary art sites.

D10

Kyoto or Osaka finale

Kyoto craft experience or Osaka Nakanoshima architecture and museum district. Closing dinner in the evening.

D11

Depart from Kansai

Transfer to Kansai International Airport. Tokyo return can be added as an extension if needed.

Included

  • Tour leader and specialized local support.
