Overview

Winter Japan with enough skiing to feel complete.

This is a complete winter route, not a simple city tour with one snow day added. The route combines four full ski days, the memorable sight of snow monkeys at Jigokudani, onsen culture, and Tokyo time for shopping or light sightseeing.

It is well suited for families and teen groups that want Japan snow but need help with snow school, equipment, transport, and safe group pacing.

Best fit

  • Families trying Japanese skiing for the first time.
  • Beginner and intermediate skiers who want structured support.
  • Teen winter camps and school holiday groups.
  • Not ideal for travelers who want no physical activity.

Highlights

Complete winter experiences with clear family support.

Four Ski Days

Long enough for beginners to truly improve and for intermediate skiers to enjoy the mountain.

Snow Monkeys

Jigokudani creates a powerful visual story for brochures, short videos, and parent-facing materials.

Onsen Recovery

Hot springs help the itinerary feel restorative, not just athletic, and support non-skiing family members.

Tokyo Gateway

Tokyo arrival and departure make the journey easier to organize for international families.

Snow School Ready

Beginner lessons, level grouping, and equipment checks can be built into the operating plan.

Family-Friendly Alternatives

Snow play, snowshoeing, sledding, shopping, or onsen time can support mixed-ability groups.

Day by day

10-day sample itinerary.

D1

Arrive in Tokyo

Private transfer, check-in, and a short briefing on winter conditions, equipment, and group safety rules.

D2

Tokyo preparation day

Light sightseeing in Asakusa, Shibuya, or Meiji Jingu. Afternoon equipment shopping or rental preparation.

D3

Nagano and snow monkeys

Take the train to Nagano, visit Jigokudani Yaen-Koen, then continue to Hakuba or Nozawa Onsen.

D4

Ski day 1

Level assessment, beginner snow school, intermediate orientation, and evening onsen recovery.

D5

Ski day 2

Continue lessons or free ski by ability group. Add family photos, mountain lunch, and local hot pot dinner.

D6

Ski day 3

Beginner turning and braking practice, longer runs for intermediate skiers, or alternate snow activities for non-skiers.

D7

Ski day 4

Final ski session, optional certificate moment, group closing dinner, and onsen time.

D8

Yuzawa or GALA option

Experience another accessible ski area, then return to Tokyo or stay in a Yuzawa onsen hotel depending on flights.

D9

Tokyo free day

Shopping, Akihabara, Ginza, Odaiba, Shibuya, or a relaxed family city route.

D10

Depart from Tokyo

Airport transfer and departure.

Included

  • Tour leader and local Japan support.
  • Hotels, onsen stays, or ski resort accommodation.
  • Breakfast and selected group meals.
  • Core transfers and snow monkey visit.
  • Ski school or instructor arrangements can be quoted.

Notes

  • Ski equipment and lift tickets should be itemized clearly.
  • Travel medical insurance with snow sport coverage is strongly recommended.
  • Weather may require operational adjustment.
  • Advanced ski coaching requires a separate quote.
